Premise

Imagine a world without God. One student who cannot accept reality can become a threat to the entire class. He might harm his classmates whenever triggered, or simply because he is sensitive to the noises they make. Who is he? What kind of student is he? This is his story.

He was once a sensible, friendly, and cheerful student during Grades 7 and 8. However, one mistake — bumping into the wrong person — led to being forbidden from joining his katropa. In his new section, his eyes blaze with fire against his classmates. He turned his new section, 9-Golf, into his enemy. There is no understanding, and no one cares. He has absolutely lost control.

Chapters

Chapter 1: The Good Old Days

Bobby spent most of his time with his katropa during Grades 7 and 8. Life was cheerful, friendships were strong, and he felt a sense of belonging that he would soon lose.

Chapter 2: The Separation

During the enrollment for Grade 9, Bobby was placed in Section 9-Golf while his katropa were assigned to 9-India. The sudden separation left him feeling isolated and frustrated.

Chapter 3: Initial Struggles

The school year 2017-2018 began, and Bobby struggled to adjust to his new section. He found it hard to connect with his new classmates and often felt lonely. Until one day, an incident changed everything. Bobby accidentally bumped into Clarisse Mei Tan of 9-India, causing a scene that would have lasting repercussions.

Chapter 4: The Fire Burns in His Eyes

After the incident with Clarisse, Bobby's anger and frustration grew. He began to lash out at his classmates and struggled to control his emotions.

Chapter 5: Remediation

Due to Bobby's increasingly aggressive and violent behavior, Janelle Marie Reyes, the class president of 9-Golf, as well as their adviser, Sir Eliott Benitez, decided to take action by telling her classmates to avoid Bobby as much as possible for their own safety. Furthermore, they offered 9-Golf classmates the opportunity to sit in with 9-Hotel. In this case, Sir Benitez would still be their adviser, and would still be part of 9-Golf.

Chapter 6: The Breaking Point

By March 2018, Bobby's behavior was out of control. He had become a danger to himself and the 9-Golf section, and the school community was on edge. Until at one point of the month, Bobby reached his breaking point, leading to a tragic incident that would cause the deaths of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado.

Chapter 7: The Hospital Rush

Following the stabbing incident, Amelia and Charlotte were rushed to the hospital. The school community was left in shock and mourning as they grappled with the aftermath of the tragedy. However, both Amelia and Charlotte died in the emergency room shortly after arriving.

Chapter 8: The Aftermath

In the wake of the tragedy, the school community struggled to come to terms with the loss of Amelia and Charlotte. Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario, of Grade 10-Lima, who was Amelia's boyfriend, visited the hospital morgue to see her one last time before her funeral.

Chapter 9: The Investigation

Authorities and investigators launched a thorough investigation into the stabbing incident. They interviewed witnesses, reviewed security footage, and gathered evidence to understand what led to the tragedy.

Chapter 10: The Wakes of Amelia and Charlotte

The wakes of both Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado were held at their respective family homes. Family members, friends, classmates, and teachers gathered to pay their last respects and bid farewell to the two young lives lost too soon.

Chapter 11: Bobby Goes to DSWD

After the incident, Bobby was taken into custody and later transferred to the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) for rehabilitation and counseling.

Chapter 12: Grieving Families

Families of the victims, Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado, struggled to cope with their loss. They even recalled the happy memories they shared with their daughters before the tragic incident.

Chapter 13: The Final Exams

The school reopened for the final exams of the school year 2017-2018, but the atmosphere was somber and heavy with grief. Students and teachers alike struggled to focus on their studies while coping with the aftermath of the tragedy.

Chapter 14: Farewell, Amelia and Charlotte

The school community came together to bid farewell to Amelia and Charlotte in a heartfelt memorial service. Students, teachers, and family members shared their memories and stories, celebrating the lives of the two young girls. Furthermore, they were laid to rest in a peaceful cemetery, where their loved ones could visit and remember them.

Chapter 15: The Final Exams, part 2

Before the closing party for the school year 2017-2018 in the afternoon of March 19, 2018, the final exams for the remaining subjects were held in the morning.

Chapter 16: Tributes

That afternoon, during the closing party for the school year 2017-2018, tributes were made in honor of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. Students and teachers shared heartfelt messages, poems, and songs to remember and celebrate the lives of the two young girls. Bobby's katropa also said sorry to what happened to Amelia and Charlotte. Bobby killed the two girls because he was unable to control his emotions after being separated from his katropa.

Chapter 17: The Commencement Exercises of 2018

The commencement exercises for the school year 2017-2018 were held, marking the end of a challenging year for the school community. In the morning of March 23, 2018, the recognition rites for Grades 7-9 took place, where Amelia Nicole Santos was posthumously awarded Fourth Honors, Best in English, Best in Filipino, and Best in Araling Panlipunan. Amelia's younger brother, Ryder Miguel Santos, of Grade 7-Alpha, was awarded Seventh Honors during the same event. In the afternoon, the moving up ceremony for Grade 10 students was held, where Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario, Amelia's boyfriend from 10-Lima, graduated from Junior High School.

Chapter 18: A Fresh New Start

The school year 2018-2019 began, marking a fresh new start for the school community. Most students of 9-Golf, as well as some from 9-Hotel were classmates again in 10-Juliett, with Ms. Ma. Alyssandra Villanueva, their Filipino teacher, as their new adviser.

Chapter 19: Lawrence's Dream

Lawrence Allan Solinap of 10-Juliett, who once befriended Bobby during Grade 9, had a dream about Bobby being reunited with his katropa in Grade 10-Lima, with Sir Joey Tañada as their adviser.

Chapter 20: Undas

Grade 10-Juliett and Ms. Villanueva visited the cemetery during Undas (All Saints' Day) to pay their respects to Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. They brought flowers and offered prayers for the two girls, remembering their lives and the impact they had on the school community. Furthermore, the families of Amelia and Charlotte, as well as Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario, Amelia's former boyfriend, also visited the cemetery to honor their loved ones.

Chapter 21: Christmas Party / A Supposed Sweet Sixteen

The Christmas party was held, bringing joy and celebration to the students and teachers, on December 21, 2018 after their 3rd Unit Test. After the party, most of the 10-Juliett students went to the cemetery to visit the grave of Amelia Nicole Santos, as it was also her supposed sweet sixteen birthday.

Chapter 22: One Year Later

One year after the tragic incident, the school community held a tribute to remember Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. Students and teachers reflected on the lessons learned from the tragedy and emphasized the importance of empathy, mental health awareness, and community support. The class presidents of all three Grade 10 classes (Juliett, Kilo, and Lima) led the tribute, honoring the memories of Amelia and Charlotte, with their class presidents Janelle Marie Reyes (10-Juliett), Mark Anthony Baes (10-Kilo), and Patrick Alarcon (10-Lima) giving speeches about them. They are joined by their advisers, Ms. Ma. Alyssandra Villanueva (10-Juliett), Ms. Marilyn Espineda (10-Kilo), and Sir Joey Tañada (10-Lima).

Chapter 23: The Commencement Exercises of 2019

The commencement exercises for the graduating class of 2019 were held at the school auditorium, celebrating the achievements of the students. Family, friends, and faculty gathered to honor the graduates, with speeches highlighting their hard work and dedication throughout the years. The event was filled with emotions as students prepared to embark on new journeys after graduation. Janelle Marie Reyes, the Batch Valedictorian of 2019 and former class president of 9-Golf and 10-Juliett, delivered the valedictory address, inspiring her fellow graduates to pursue their dreams and make a positive impact in the world. She even honored the memories of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado in her speech. Bobby's katropa from 10-Lima also graduated that day together, still honoring Bobby in their hearts.

Chapter 24: Bobby Undergoes Therapy

Ms. Teresa Viloria, a social worker from the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD), conducted therapy sessions with Bobby to help him cope with his emotions and understand the consequences of his actions. Furthermore, he even had delusions of his katropa, claiming that they were here for him.

Chapter 25: Eighteen Candles, Unlit

There was no celebration for Amelia Nicole Santos's eighteenth birthday, as she had passed away in 2018. On December 21, 2020, her family and friends gathered at her grave to remember her and honor her memory, including her former classmates and teachers from the school. Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario, Amelia's former boyfriend, also visited her grave to pay his respects.

Chapter 26: The Need for Justice

The families of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado were still grieving their loss and were longing for justice for the death of their daughters. They worked with legal authorities to ensure that Bobby was held accountable for his actions and that justice was served.

Chapter 27: The Trial

The court trial for Bobby Ramirez took place, where evidence was presented, and testimonies were heard. The legal proceedings aimed to determine Bobby's culpability and the appropriate consequences for his actions in the stabbing incident that led to the deaths of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado in March 2018. Furthermore, the trial was closely followed by the school community and the families of the victims, who sought closure and justice for their loved ones. In this trial, Bobby was found guilty of double homicide and was sentenced to life imprisonment without parole.

Chapter 28: A World Behind The Walls

Bobby began his life sentence in a high-security prison, where he faced the challenges of adapting to life behind bars. In the New Bilibid Prison, he was assigned in Dormitory 6, Section B, which housed inmates convicted of serious crimes. There, he met Kuya Manny, the dorm's mayor who became his mentor and guide in navigating the harsh realities of prison life. Kuya Manny also introduced Bobby to his fellow inmates. Benjo, a former construction worker convicted of drug trafficking; Lito, a petty thief who stole a motorcycle, and Tony, who swindled people of Php 483,300 through online scams. Kuya Manny asked Bobby what crime he committed. Bobby silently replied, "Double homicide." The inmates were shocked to hear that. They beat him up and mocked him for being a killer. However, Kuya Manny intervened and told them to leave Bobby alone. That night, Kuya Manny asked Bobby why he committed such a crime. Bobby explained his story, from being separated from his katropa to the tragic incident that led to the deaths of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. He then initiated Bobby by cleaning the dormitory.

Chapter 29: Dormitory 6-B / Bobby Had A Nightmare

Bobby's fellow inmates, Benjo, Lito, and Tony, shared their stories with him, revealing the circumstances that led to their incarceration. Benjo talked about his involvement in drug trafficking, Lito shared his experience of stealing a motorcycle, and Tony recounted how he swindled people through online scams. One night, Bobby had a nightmare about Clarisse Mei Tan tormenting him not to get near to his katropa. He woke up in a cold sweat, feeling anxious and fearful.

Chapter 30: The Interrogation

Bobby was summoned for an interrogation by the prison authorities regarding the 2018 stabbing incident. During the interrogation, he was questioned about the events leading up to the incident, his motives, and his mental state at the time. Authorities drowned him in water to force a confession out of him, but Bobby refused to give in. Despite the pressure, Bobby maintained his innocence and explained his side of the story.

Chapter 31: Lunch Time

During lunch time at the mess hall, Bobby ate sinigang na baboy with rice. However, one inmate poured gasoline on Bobby's food. This reminded him of how Clarisse forbade him from entering 9-India. He then caused trouble by throwing his food at the inmates, leading to a chaotic scene in the mess hall and therfore initiating a riot among the inmates.

Chapter 32: Meeting the Psychiatrist

Bobby was referred to psychiatrist Dr. Antonio Vinzon, Jr. for evaluation. During the session, he opened up about his feelings of anger, betrayal, and confusion. The psychiatrist listened attentively and asked probing questions to help Bobby explore his emotions and thoughts. Through their conversations, Bobby began to understand the root causes of his behavior and the impact of his past experiences.

Chapter 33: Visitors

Bobby was visited by Clarisse Mei Tan, now a Nursing student, who came to forgive him for forbidding him from entering 9-India, which became the root of his anger and frustration. He was also visited by Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario, who came to forgive Bobby for the death of Amelia Nicole Santos. Joining Anjhelo is his new girlfriend, Stacey Anne Degamo, who is a Psychology student.

Chapter 34: Bobby Turns 19 in Prison

Bobby was harshly greeted by his fellow inmates on his 19th birthday in prison on April 20, 2022. They mocked him for being a killer and reminded him of the lives he took away. They even threw eggs and rotten food at him, making his birthday a miserable experience. They also even sang "Happy Birthday" in a mocking tone, and used plywood to hit him. Bobby felt a mix of anger and sadness, realizing that he was truly alone in this fight.

Chapter 35: The Raid at Dorm 6-B

On June 27, 2022, a raid was conducted at Dormitory 6-B of the New Bilibid Prison, where Bobby was housed. Authorities discovered marijuana gummy bears and shabu (methamphetamine) hidden in various locations within the dormitory. Furthermore, they found a PlayStation 5 console, a Nintendo Switch console, and a gaming laptop, which were allegedly smuggled into the prison for the inmates' use. The raid resulted in multiple arrests and heightened security measures within the prison.

Chapter 36: Rumors of Release

On October 2022, rumors began circulating that Bobby might be released from prison soon. Atty. Gia Sevilla, a well-known lawyer, was reportedly handling his case and working towards securing his release. However, teachers and some classmates from his previous school opposed his release, demanding justice for the victims, Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. However, Clarisse Mei Tan, as well as Lawrence Allan Solinap and his katropa, supported Bobby's release, believing that he deserved a second chance at life. In light of this, Janelle Marie Reyes, now a 2nd Year Computer Science student, organized a protest rally to voice their opposition against Bobby's potential release.

Chapter 37: The Protest

On October 28, 2022, Janelle Marie Reyes led a protest rally in Greenfield Eastern University to oppose Bobby's potential release from prison. Students, teachers, and community members gathered to voice their concerns and demand justice for Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. 12,000 students, teachers, and activists joined the protest, holding placards and chanting slogans calling for accountability and justice.

Chapter 38: The Plebiscite

On November 7, 2022, a plebiscite was held to determine whether Bobby Ramirez should be released from prison early. The school community, including students, teachers, and staff, participated in the voting process to express their opinions on the matter.

Chapter 39: The Results

That night of November 7, 2022, the results of the plebiscite were announced. The majority voted against Bobby's release, citing concerns about justice for the victims and the safety of the school community. As a result, Bobby remained in prison to serve out his life sentence.

Chapter 40: The Aftermath of the Plebiscite

Several members of the community expressed their reactions to the plebiscite results. Some were relieved that Bobby would remain in prison, believing it was the right decision for justice. Others, however, felt that the decision was unjust and called for further action to support Bobby's release.

Chapter 41: Bobby's Continued Struggles in Prison

Bobby faced numerous challenges while incarcerated. He struggled to adapt to prison life, dealing with the harsh realities of his environment. Despite the support from some community members, Bobby felt isolated and alone in his fight for justice.

Chapter 42: A Formidable Foe

Bobby encountered a formidable foe in prison, an inmate named Harold Puentespina, who was known for his aggressive behavior and dominance over other inmates. Harold saw Bobby as a threat and sought to assert his dominance over him. The two clashed on multiple occasions, leading to tense confrontations and escalating conflicts within the prison. Like Bobby, Harold was also convicted of murder.

Chapter 43: Bobby's Behavior Worsened

Bobby's behavior in prison began to deteriorate as he struggled to cope with the challenges he faced. He would often lash out at other inmates and staff, leading to disciplinary actions and further isolation. Bobby's mental health continued to decline, and he found it increasingly difficult to manage his anger and frustration. Dr. Antonio Vinzon, Jr., the prison psychologist, was assigned to help Bobby cope with his issues.

Chapter 44: Anjhelo Missed His Former Girlfriend Amelia So Much

Anjhelo found himself reminiscing about his former girlfriend, Amelia, and the life they once shared. He missed her companionship and the love they had, which felt like a distant memory. As he navigated the challenges of losing a loved one before, Anjhelo often thought about how different things could have been if Bobby had not taken Amelia's life. The weight of his regrets began to take a toll on his mental health, and he sought solace in the memories of happier times. Despite these, Stacey Anne Degamo, his current girlfriend, provided him with support and comfort during these difficult moments.

Chapter 45: The Grieving Families Seek Justice

The families of Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado continued to seek justice for their daughters. They even went to church together to pray for strength and guidance in their pursuit of justice. Ryder Miguel Santos, Amelia's younger brother, also joined them in their quest for justice. Ryder prayed for his sister's soul and for the strength to support his family during this difficult time. He even prayed for Bobby, hoping that he would suffer the consequences of his actions.

Chapter 46: The Last Stand

On February 2, 2024, during dinner time at the mess hall, a violent prison riot occurred after someone cut through the queue for food. Tensions escalated quickly, leading to a full-blown riot among the inmates. Bobby found himself caught in the chaos, trying to navigate through the violence and confusion. He saw Sister Grace Montoya, a nun who visited the prison for a religious outreach program, in which he held her hostage to use her as a human shield. Prison guards and authorities worked to regain control of the situation, but not before several inmates were injured. They asked Bobby to release Sister Grace and surrender peacefully, but he refused. The standoff continued, with Bobby using Sister Grace as leverage to negotiate for better treatment and conditions in the prison. However, when the nun's life was threatened, authorities had no choice but to take decisive action. In the end, Bobby was shot and killed by the prison guards, bringing an end to the violent riot, as well as to his troubled life. The nun was rescued unharmed, and the prison returned to a state of order. At the age of 20, Bobby's life came to a tragic end in a bloody prison riot. It was Willie Fernando who shot Bobby dead.

Epilogue: Justice Finally Served

Ryder's prayers were finally answered when justice was served for Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado. Bobby's death in the prison riot brought closure to the grieving families, who could finally find peace knowing that justice had been served. They continued to honor the memories of their daughters, cherishing the moments they shared and finding solace in the knowledge that justice had been achieved. They even visited Amelia's and Charlotte's graves together to pay their respects and remember their loved ones. They are joined by Anjhelo Mikael Del Rosario and his new girlfriend Stacey Anne Degamo, who also paid their respects to Amelia, as well as Janelle Marie Reyes, now a 3rd Year Computer Science student, who came to honor both Amelia and Charlotte. Willie Fernando, the prison guard who brought justice to the deaths of Amelia and Charlotte by shooting Bobby dead, also met with the families of Amelia and Charlotte to assure them that justice had been served. The family of Amelia Nicole Santos even invited Anjhelo and Janelle to their home for a simple meal as a gesture of gratitude for their support during their time of grief. There, they shared stories and memories of Amelia, finding comfort in each other's company. The family has also adapted pet cat named "Amelia" in honor of their beloved daughter.

Bonus Chapter: Bobby's Wake

Bobby Ramirez's wake was held at a trash dump site. Most of them laughed at Bobby's funeral, mocking him for being a killer and a disgrace to society. He was naked only in his underwear and placed in a makeshift wooden box, symbolizing his downfall from a troubled youth to a convicted criminal. People were free to laugh at him, as they believed that he deserved it for the pain and suffering he caused to Amelia Nicole Santos and Charlotte Anne Delgado, as well as their families. Erin Antonette Lao, now a 3rd Year Medical Biology student, whose ukulele was destroyed by Bobby in Grade 9, even played a mocking song for Bobby during his wake. His remains were later thrown into a garbage truck and dumped in a landfill, symbolizing the end of his troubled life and the consequences of his actions.
